# Getting Help and Next Steps

Welcome to the guide that helps you move forward after setting up and running your first tests with GoogleTest. This page points you toward official documentation, community support, and recommendations for deepening your knowledge or resolving challenges beyond initial usage.

---

## 1. Accessing Official Documentation

GoogleTest and GoogleMock come with comprehensive official documentation designed to guide users from beginners to experts.

- **GoogleTest User's Guide**: The core guide covering writing tests, basic and advanced concepts, and usage patterns. Start here for an in-depth understanding of test creation and execution.
- **Mocking Reference and Guides**: Detailed explanations and examples for creating mock classes, setting expectations, and controlling behaviors using GoogleMock.
- **gMock for Dummies**: A beginner-friendly introduction to mocking with practical examples.
- **Cookbook and Cheat Sheet**: Recipes and quick references for common mocking use cases, patterns, matchers, and actions.

## 2. Community and Support Resources

Beyond official docs, you can tap into the vibrant open-source community and other help avenues:

- **GitHub Repository Issues and Discussions:** Report bugs, feature requests, or find answers to common questions.
- **Google Testing Blog:** Read posts for best practices, advanced tips, and community announcements.

**Tip:** Before raising new issues, consult the FAQ and existing issue threads to find quicker resolutions.

## 3. Getting Help Effectively

When seeking help, keep these tips in mind:

- Clearly describe your environment, platform, and exact GoogleTest version.
- Provide minimal reproducible examples of your test code or mock setup to illustrate issues.
- Share error messages, compiler warnings, or runtime output verbatim.
- When requesting features or reporting bugs, detail expected behavior versus observed behavior.

These practices help you and maintainers solve problems faster and more accurately.

## 4. Useful Tips and Next Steps

- **Expand Your Skills:** After mastering setup and running your first tests, explore advanced topics:

## Troubleshooting

**If You're Stuck:**

- Confirm you have followed the installation, configuration, and basic testing steps correctly.
- Check compiler and environment compatibility as described in Supported Platforms.
- Use `--gmock_verbose=info` to get detailed logs of mock interactions.
- Consult the [Common Setup Issues and Solutions](../getting-started/troubleshooting-support/common-issues-fixes) page.

**If your test expectations are not met:**

- Ensure your `EXPECT_CALL` precedes any invocation of the mock methods.
- Review ordering constraints such as `InSequence` or `After` clauses to avoid unexpected call ordering.

**When in doubt, use the community and official support channels for direct assistance.**
