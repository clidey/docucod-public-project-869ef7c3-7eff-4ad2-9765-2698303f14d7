# Getting Help and Next Steps

Welcome to the final stage of your GoogleTest onboarding journey. This page points you to essential community resources, ways to deepen your understanding, and how to engage with fellow users and contributors. Whether you're troubleshooting, building advanced tests, or looking to contribute, these directions will ignite your growth and mastery.

## 1. Accessing Support and Community Resources

When you encounter challenges or want to expand your expertise, the GoogleTest community and documentation are here to support you.

### Where to Find Help:

- **Community-Created Documentation**
  - Explore guides and tutorials contributed by users worldwide to gain fresh insights and practical advice.
  - Visit the community documentation index: [Community Documentation](https://github.com/google/googletest/blob/main/docs/community_created_documentation.md)
  - Example resource: [Googlemock Insights](https://github.com/ElectricRCAircraftGuy/eRCaGuy_dotfiles/blob/master/googletest/insights.md) by ElectricRCAircraftGuy

- **Official GoogleTest Documentation**
  - Review comprehensive references and guides available on the official site.
  - Navigate through the documentation sections using the [GoogleTest Navigation Map](https://google.github.io/googletest/).

- **Issue Tracker & Source Repository**
  - Report bugs or request features on the GitHub repository: [GoogleTest Repo](https://github.com/google/googletest)
  - Browse closed issues for solutions to common problems.

### Tips for Effective Support Requests

- Clearly describe your problem context, including platform, compiler, and GoogleTest version.
- Include code snippets or test samples that reproduce the issue.
- Share the exact error message or behavior encountered.

## 4. Troubleshooting & Common Issues

While working with GoogleTest, you may encounter some challenges. Here are quick tips to keep your workflow smooth:

### Common Areas to Check:

- **Installation Problems**
  - Ensure supported compilers and platforms are used as per [System Requirements & Supported Platforms](https://google.github.io/googletest/getting-started/prerequisites-and-installation/system-requirements.html).
  - Follow installation instructions closely; refer to [Installation Methods](https://google.github.io/googletest/getting-started/prerequisites-and-installation/installation-methods.html).

- **Linking and Build Issues**
  - Verify library linking order and inclusion of necessary source files.
  - Consult the [Troubleshooting Installation & Build Problems](https://google.github.io/googletest/getting-started/support-and-troubleshooting/troubleshooting-setup.html) guide.

- **Unexpected Test Behavior**
  - Increase verbosity using command line flags like `--gtest_verbose` or `--gmock_verbose`.
  - Use mock debugging tips from the [Mocking Cookbook](https://google.github.io/googletest/gmock_cook_book.html#Gaining-Super-Vision-into-Mock-Calls).

- **Consult FAQ**
  - Common questions and their answers can save time: [GoogleTest FAQ](https://google.github.io/googletest/faq.html)
