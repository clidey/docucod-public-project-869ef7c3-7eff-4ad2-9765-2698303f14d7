# Version Overview

The Version Overview page offers a concise and clear summary of GoogleTest's major and minor releases, along with their release dates. Designed to provide users with a quick snapshot of the project's release cadence and significant milestones, this page helps you identify when important features, enhancements, and fixes were introduced.

---

## Why This Matters to You

Understanding when specific versions of GoogleTest were released—including the key improvements they introduced—enables you to:

- **Make informed upgrade decisions**, ensuring your testing frameworks benefit from the latest capabilities and bug fixes.
- **Identify relevant changes** that may affect your testing strategies or integration with other tools.
- **Plan development cycles** around stable or feature-rich releases suited to your projects.

---

## What You’ll Find on This Page

The Version Overview distills the essential information about GoogleTest's release history:

- **Release Dates:** When each version became available.
- **Version Types:** Differentiation between major and minor releases.
- **Key Improvements:** Highlights of new functionality, enhancements, and fixes.
- **Milestones:** Important shifts such as upgrades in language standards or dependencies.

### Practical Use Case

Suppose you rely on GoogleTest for your C++ project and want to upgrade from version 1.16 to 1.17. This page can quickly tell you the release date of 1.17 and the major updates it offers, such as requiring C++17 and possible API changes. You can then check how those changes align with your project needs and compatibility.

Since this page serves as a high-level summary, detailed notes and instructions for each release are found on the **Detailed Release Notes** page. Use the navigation menu to explore:

- Other changelog sections that address **breaking changes, upgrade instructions, and deprecations**.
