# Support Resources and Community Channels

This page guides you on the official avenues for getting help, reporting bugs, contributing, and connecting with the vibrant community around GoogleTest and GoogleMock. Leveraging these resources will ensure productive use of the frameworks and smooth resolution of issues.

---

## Official Documentation

Comprehensive, authoritative information is available through the official documentation. It covers everything from introduction and setup to advanced mocking and integration.

- **Primary User Guide:** The complete GoogleTest User's Guide can be found [here](https://github.com/google/googletest/tree/main/docs). It includes beginner tutorials, advanced techniques, and API references.
- **Mocking Reference:** For detailed instructions on creating and managing mocks, see the [Mocking Reference](https://github.com/google/googletest/blob/main/docs/reference/mocking.md).
- **gMock for Dummies:** A beginner-friendly guide to mocking fundamentals and best practices is available in [gMock for Dummies](https://google.github.io/googletest/gmock_for_dummies.html).
- **gMock Cookbook:** This contains real-world recipes and tips for advanced mocking patterns at [gMock Cookbook](https://google.github.io/googletest/gmock_cook_book.html).
- **Cheat Sheets:** Quick references for matchers, actions, and expectations are provided in [Mocking Cheat Sheet](https://google.github.io/googletest/gmock_cheat_sheet.html).

These documents are curated to help users from first-timers to experts achieve mastery with GoogleTest and GoogleMock.

---

## Getting Help

When you encounter questions or issues, consider the following support options:

### Issue Reporting and Bug Tracking

- The [GoogleTest GitHub repository](https://github.com/google/googletest) is the official place to report bugs and suggest features.
- Before opening a new issue, search existing issues to avoid duplicates and gather relevant context.

### Community Forums and Discussions

- Although there is no dedicated official forum, developers commonly use programming community sites such as Stack Overflow, tagging questions with `googletest` or `gmock`.
- Check and contribute to community-created documentation and guides like [Googlemock Insights](https://github.com/ElectricRCAircraftGuy/eRCaGuy_dotfiles/blob/master/googletest/insights.md) for alternative perspectives.

### Mailing Lists and Chat Channels

- There are no official mailing lists or chat channels maintained by the GoogleTest team; however, various open-source and C++ community platforms may have informal discussion groups.

---

## Contributing to GoogleTest and GoogleMock

GoogleTest and GoogleMock welcome contributions from the community. Here's how to get involved:

- **Source Repository:** Explore and fork the [GitHub repository](https://github.com/google/googletest).
- **Contribution Guidelines:** Follow the established contribution workflow, coding style, and testing requirements detailed in the repository's README.
- **Code Reviews:** Participate in pull requests and code reviews to help improve the project.
- **Documentation:** Improve, update, or add documentation based on your experience and findings.

Making meaningful contributions helps keep the project robust and beneficial for all users.

---

## Best Practices for Seeking Support

- Before asking for help, ensure you have updated to the latest stable release and reviewed the official documentation and troubleshooting guides.
- Provide clear, concise descriptions of your issue or question, along with relevant code snippets or error messages.
- Use the recommended logging and verbose flags (`--gmock_verbose`, `--gtest_stack_trace_depth`) to generate detailed diagnostic information.

## Additional Tips

- Enable verbose output using `--gmock_verbose=info` to diagnose mock call matching and expectation issues.
- Use `NiceMock` and `StrictMock` to adjust how uninteresting calls are treated, aiding test clarity and troubleshooting.
- Review related pages on [Installation and Setup Errors](faq/getting-started-and-common-issues/installation-setup-errors) and [Mocking and Integration Errors](faq/advanced-usage-and-integration/mocking-integration-errors) for troubleshooting common pitfalls.
