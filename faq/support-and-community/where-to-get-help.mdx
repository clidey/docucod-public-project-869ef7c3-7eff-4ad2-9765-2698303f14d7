# Where Can I Get Help or Report Issues?

This page guides you to valuable community and official resources for obtaining help or reporting issues related to GoogleTest and GoogleMock. It outlines where to find support, how to search for existing answers, and what information to provide to receive the fastest, most effective assistance.

---

## Community Forums

Engage with fellow users and contributors through the official forums where you can ask questions, share insights, and discover common solutions:

- **GoogleTest Discussions on GitHub**: Online discussions hosted with issue tracking and community participation.
- **Stack Overflow**: Use tags like [`googletest`](https://stackoverflow.com/questions/tagged/googletest) and [`googlemock`](https://stackoverflow.com/questions/tagged/googlemock) to find and post questions.

When posting on forums, please:

- Include a **clear, minimal reproducible example** of your code or test failure.
- Describe your setup including **platform, compiler, and GoogleTest/GoogleMock versions**.
- Provide details on error messages or unexpected behavior.

This improves your chance for quick and accurate responses.

---

## Issue Trackers

To report bugs or suggest enhancements for GoogleTest/GoogleMock:

- Visit the **[GoogleTest GitHub Issue Tracker](https://github.com/google/googletest/issues)**
- When submitting an issue, please:
  - Provide a **concise summary** of the problem.
  - Include **steps to reproduce** the issue.
  - Attach relevant **code snippets or logs**.
  - Specify your **environment details** (OS, compiler, version).

Before filing, search existing issues to avoid duplicates.

## How to Search for Existing Answers

To save time and find faster solutions, use the following strategies:

- Use precise keywords and error message snippets in search engines.
- Consult the FAQ and troubleshooting pages to check if your problem is addressed.
- Search GitHub issues with filters on labels, keywords, and status.
- Review discussions and pull requests for insights into recent fixes or workarounds.

Often, your issue has already been encountered and resolved.

---

## What Information to Provide When Seeking Help

Supplying detailed and relevant context enables helpers to diagnose issues accurately and respond quickly. When reporting problems or asking questions, include:

- **A minimal reproducible example** of your code or test setup demonstrating the issue.
- Your **platform and environment details**, including OS, compiler version, and GoogleTest/GoogleMock version.
- Exact **error messages or test outputs**.
- Steps you have taken to reproduce or troubleshoot the problem.
- Any relevant **build system or configuration snippets**.

Avoid sharing large unrelated code or internal implementation details.

## Getting Official Support

GoogleTest and GoogleMock are open-source projects primarily supported through community collaboration and documentation. There is no official commercial support, but you can:

- Engage via the GitHub repository for help.
- Monitor the project’s **release notes and changelog** for updates and resolved issues.

If you work within a company using GoogleTest and require professional support, consider reaching out to consulting services specializing in C++ testing frameworks.
