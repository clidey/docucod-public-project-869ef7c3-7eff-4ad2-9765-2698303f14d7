# Where Can I Get Additional Help or Support?

This page serves as your comprehensive directory for finding assistance with GoogleTest and GoogleMock. Whether you're a new user needing quick answers, a developer troubleshooting integration issues, or an advanced user seeking expert guidance, here you’ll find the official documentation, community forums, and issue trackers to help you move forward swiftly and confidently.

---

## Official Documentation

The primary source for all GoogleTest and GoogleMock information is the official documentation. It covers everything from installation, writing tests, mocking features, advanced integration, and troubleshooting.

- [GoogleTest Documentation](https://google.github.io/googletest/) — The official home of comprehensive guides and API references.
- [GoogleMock Documentation](https://google.github.io/googletest/gmock_for_dummies.html) — A beginner-friendly introduction to mocking with GoogleMock, including examples and workflows.

### Key Documentation Areas to Explore

- **Getting Started & Setup**: Helpful tutorials on installation, configuration, and your first tests.
- **Mocking Reference**: Extensive coverage of mock classes, `EXPECT_CALL`, `ON_CALL`, matchers, and actions.
- **Guides and Cookbooks**: Practical recipes and best practices for effective test design and mocking.
- **FAQ & Troubleshooting**: Solutions for common errors, linking problems, and usage questions.


## Community and Discussion Forums

Joining the community can offer access to a wealth of user experiences, advice, and shared solutions. Community forums both official and third-party provide venues to ask questions and discuss GoogleTest/GoogleMock usage.

- **Google Groups**: The [googletest-users](https://groups.google.com/g/googletest-users) mailing list is a popular choice for asking questions and following development discussions.
- **Stack Overflow**: Use the tags [`googletest`](https://stackoverflow.com/questions/tagged/googletest) and [`google-mock`](https://stackoverflow.com/questions/tagged/google-mock) for diverse Q&A contributed by the global developer community.

### Tips for Engaging With the Community

- Search existing questions before asking to avoid duplicates.
- Include minimal reproducible examples and error messages for quicker help.
- Stay engaged and share your solutions once your question is resolved.

## Issue Trackers and Bug Reporting

If you encounter bugs, missing features, or have suggestions for improvements, reporting them enables the maintainers to address these issues efficiently.

- **GitHub Issue Tracker:**
  - Report issues at the official repository: [https://github.com/google/googletest/issues](https://github.com/google/googletest/issues)
  - When reporting, provide detailed descriptions, reproduction steps, platform and compiler versions, and any relevant code snippets.

### Best Practices When Filing an Issue

- Confirm your issue is reproducible with the latest version.
- Review existing issues to check if it’s already reported.
- Use clear, concise language and include build logs or error output.

## Third-Party Resources and Tutorials

Many community-created tutorials, blog posts, and videos cover GoogleTest and GoogleMock topics:

- Blogs and articles that focus on test-driven development with GoogleTest.
- YouTube channels that provide step-by-step walkthroughs and demonstrations.
- Open source projects or examples demonstrating best mocking practices.

While these are not officially maintained, they can provide supplementary perspectives and practical insights.

## Practical Tips for Getting Support

- **Start from the official docs** — they are comprehensive and up-to-date.
- **Use command-line flags like `--gmock_verbose`** to get more detailed output for debugging.
- **Isolate your problem with minimal test cases** before asking for help.
- **Leverage community forums early** — many common issues have already been solved.
- When asking for help, **always mention your platform, compiler version, GoogleTest version**, and the context.

## Troubleshooting Workflow

If you're stuck:

1. **Check official docs and guides** to ensure correct usage.
2. **Google the error or behavior** along with 'googletest' or 'gmock'.
3. **Search or ask on Stack Overflow** with reproductions and error details.
4. If it seems a bug, **file a detailed issue on GitHub**.
5. For immediate help, **join the googletest-users mailing list**.
