# Where to Get Support

This page lists the official and community support channels available for GoogleTest and GoogleMock, helping you find assistance with complex issues, bug reporting, and best practices. Whether you face challenges in setup, test writing, or debugging, these resources connect you to expert advice and active user communities.

---

## Official Support Channels

### GitHub Repository
- Access the official GoogleTest GitHub repository for reporting bugs, submitting feature requests, and browsing issues:

  [https://github.com/google/googletest](https://github.com/google/googletest)

- Use the **Issues** tab to see existing problems or create new detailed bug reports.

### Mailing List and Forums
- Subscribe to the GoogleTest forum mailing list at googletestframework@googlegroups.com for announcement and discussion of issues.
- Engage with key maintainers and community contributors to get advice and share knowledge.

### Contribution Guidelines
- Before contributing, consult the official contribution guidelines hosted in the repository for patch formats, coding standards, and review processes.
- Detailed guidelines ensure your reports or pull requests are handled efficiently.

## Community Support

### Community-Created Documentation
- Explore user-generated guides, tutorials, and examples to gain practical insights:

  - [Googlemock Insights](https://github.com/ElectricRCAircraftGuy/eRCaGuy_dotfiles/blob/master/googletest/insights.md)

- These resources complement official docs with real-world use cases and tips.

### Discussion Forums and Q&A Sites
- Many users discuss GoogleTest and GoogleMock on platforms like Stack Overflow and Reddit.
- Search for existing questions or post your own using tags `googletest` and `googlemock`.

### Tutorials and Blog Posts
- Various experts have written tutorials explaining advanced mocking, test writing patterns, and troubleshooting strategies.
- These can often be found by searching for terms like "GoogleTest mocking tutorial" or "GoogleMock best practices".

---

## How to Seek Help Effectively

- **Provide Context:** When asking for help, include details such as your platform, compiler version, GoogleTest version, and relevant code snippets.
- **Reproduce Problems:** Where possible, create minimal reproducible examples that demonstrate the issue.
- **Check Existing Resources:** Review installation guides, FAQs, and troubleshooting documents before posting.

<Tip>
Joining official and community channels increases your chances of quickly resolving issues and ensures you receive advice aligned with best practices.
</Tip>
